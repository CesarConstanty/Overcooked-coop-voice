Gameplay Basics

Movement and interactions

You can move up, down, left, and right using the arrow keys, and interact with objects using the spacebar.

You can interact with objects by facing them and pressing spacebar. Here are some examples:

  • Pick up ingredients (onions or tomatoes) by facing the ingredient area and pressing spacebar.
  • If you're holding an ingredient and face an empty counter, pressing spacebar puts the item down.
  • Face a pot and press spacebar to add an ingredient (if it's not full).
  • Face a non-empty pot while holding nothing and press spacebar to begin cooking.

You cannot occupy the same location as your partner.

Cooking

Once an ingredient is in the pot, press spacebar to start cooking. A pot can hold up to 3 ingredients.

A red number appears over the pot to indicate cook time. It counts upward until the soup is done. Each onion adds {{ current_user.config["onion_time"] }} and each tomato adds {{ current_user.config["tomato_time"] }} to the cook time. Remember that onions grant more points than tomatoes but take longer to be cooked !



Your Goal

For each game, you will have a list of recipes to make. The goal of the game is to achieve all these recipes in the shortest possible time. Please note that you should complete the recipe list as quickly as possible, but in no specific sequence. The All Orders list shows which recipes you have to realize for the game. This is important because any soup served that is not on this list will not be counted and will just waste your time.

When a soup is served at the serving counter, it is automatically validated, and disapear from the All Orders list of the recipes you have to realize during the game.

Your goal is to make all the recipes in the "All Orders" list AS FAST AS POSSIBLE.

For example, here you have to make 3 soups:

  • tomato + tomato + tomato
  • tomato + onion
  • onion + onion + tomato



Please pay VERY close attention to this part. It is AS IMPORTANT AS the game itself.



Questions



After each GAME

At the end of each game, you will be asked questions, it is very important that you understand what is asked :

1) "How much control did you use ? " : This refers to the actual influence you exerted on what happened. Think about how much of the outcome resulted from your direct actions or commands.

2) "How much control did you feel ? " : This measures your subjective experience of control, how much it felt like you were the one guiding what happened.

3) "Who is accountable for this result ? " : Accountability is about who you believe is mainly responsible for the result: you, the team as a whole, or the artificial agent involved.

Your goal is to complete the requested recipes as quickly as possible. Once the game is complete, you will see how long it took. Then, you'll evaluate how responsible you feel for that outcome.


After each BLOCK of games

At the end of each assignment block, you will be asked to complete two questionnaires about the games you have just completed.

It is VERY IMPORTANT to answer the questions SERIOUSLY for the experiment to be valid.



Course of the experiment

You will start by performing a tutorial to familiarize yourself with the interface.

You will start each block with a practice trial to learn how to play with the agent. This trial will look like other trials but we wont use the data collected.

Then you will start the experiment where you will complete 3 blocks containing 11 games each.

You will be able to take a small break between each block if you wish.

Keep in mind that every survey is to mean to be completed in a certain amount of time
