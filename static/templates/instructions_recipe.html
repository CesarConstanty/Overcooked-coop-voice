Overcooked Experiment

Cooking Game Instructions

Hello! Welcome to this video game all about... cooking! In this experiment you will play as a chef in a restaurant specialized in soups!

Your Goal

For each game, you will have a list of recipes to make. The goal of the game is to complete all these recipes in the shortest time possible. Please note that you should complete the recipe list as quickly as possible, but in no specific sequence.

the All Orders list shows which recipes you need to complete: any prepared or served soup which is not on this list will not be counted and it will make you lose time.

When a soup is served at the serving counter, it is automatically validated, and disappears from the All Orders list of the recipes you have to realize during the game.

Your goal is to make all the recipes in the All Orders lists AS FAST AS POSSIBLE.

In the example above, you have to complete 3 recipes:

  • tomato + tomato + tomato
  • tomato + onion
  • onion + onion + tomato

Gameplay Basics

Movement and interactions

You can move up, down, left, and right using the arrow keys, and interact with objects using the spacebar.

You can interact with objects by facing them and pressing spacebar. Here are some examples:

  • Pick up ingredients (onions or tomatoes) by facing the ingredient area and pressing spacebar.
  • If you are holding an ingredient and facing an empty counter, pressing spacebar puts the item down.
  • Face a pot and press spacebar to add an ingredient (if it is not full).
  • Face a non-empty pot while holding nothing and press spacebar to begin cooking.

You cannot occupy the same location as the artificial agent.

Cooking

Once an ingredient is in the pot, press spacebar to start cooking. A pot can hold up to 3 ingredients.

A red number appears over the pot to indicate the cooking time. It counts upwards until the soup is done. Each onion adds {{ onion_time }}s and each tomato adds {{ tomato_time }}s to the cooking time. Remember that onions are worth more points than tomatoes, but take longer to cook!

Once an ingredient is in the pot, you must cook it to remove it — whether or not it matches a valid order. Do not forget to hold a plate to collect the soup!

Exchange zone mechanics

You can use exchange areas to give items to the artificial agent. To do so, pick up an item with the spacebar and place it on the exchange area by pressing spacebar again.

In-game behavior

Questions

After each GAME

At the end of each game, you will be asked 3 questions, it is very important that you understand what is asked:

1) "How much control did you use?": We are interested in the sense of control that people feel for certain tasks. Some tasks require little control, such as those that are easy or well-practiced. Other tasks require full control, such as those that are difficult or new. Please here report the percentage of your maximum control that you used.

2) "How much control did you feel?": Imagine that you are cooperating with a well known friend, the cooperation might be smooth and fluid and you might feel like you are in complete control. On the contrary, with a new comer you may feel difficult to cooperate smoothly. Regardless, then, whether you are in control or not (that is not our question here) you may sometimes feel like you are in control (and hence have a high metacognition of control) or feel like you are not (and have a low metacognition of control). Please here report how much in control you were.

3) "Whose contribution made it possible to achieve this score?": Contribution is about who you believe is mainly responsible for the result: you, the team as a whole, or the artificial agent.

After each BLOCK of games

At the end of each assignment block, you will be asked to complete two questionnaires about the games you have just completed.

It is VERY IMPORTANT to answer the questions SERIOUSLY for the experimental session to be valid.

Course of the experiment

You will start by performing a tutorial to familiarize yourself with the interface.

You will start each block with two practice game to learn how to play with the agent. This game will look like other games but we will not use the data collected.

During the experiment you will complete 3 blocks containing 12 games each.

You will be able to take a small break between each block on the instruction page.

Keep in mind that every survey is meant to be completed in a limited amount of time.
