Movement and interactions

You can move up, down, left, and right using the arrow keys, and interact with objects using the spacebar.

You can interact with objects by facing them and pressing spacebar. Here are some examples:

  • You can pick up ingredients (onions or tomatoes) by facing the ingredient area and pressing spacebar.
  • If you are holding an ingredient, are facing an empty counter, and press spacebar, you put the ingredient on the counter.
  • If you are holding an ingredient, are facing a pot that is not full, and press spacebar, you will put the ingredient in the pot.
  • If you are facing a pot that is non-empty, are currently holding nothing, and and press spacebar, you will begin cooking a soup.



Note that as you and your partner are moving around the kitchen you cannot occupy the same location.



Cooking

Once an ingredient is in the pot, you can begin cooking a soup by pressing spacebar as noted above. You can place up to 3 ingredients in a pot before cooking.

When a soup begins cooking, you'll notice a red number appear over the pot to denote the cook time. This number counts upward until the soup is done. Soup cook time is determined its ingredients; every onion adds {{ onion_time }} and every tomato adds {{ tomato_time }} to the cook time.



Serving

Once the soup is in a bowl, you can serve it by bringing it to a grey serving counter.

Your Goal

For each game, you will have a list of recipes to make. The goal of the game is to achieve all these recipes in the shortest possible time. Please note that you should complete the recipe list as quickly as possible, but in no specific sequence. The All Orders list shows which recipes you have to realize for the game. This is important because any soup served that is not on this list will not be counted and will just waste your time.

When a soup is served at the serving counter, it is automatically validated, and disapear from the All Orders list of the recipes you have to realize during the game. Your goal is to make all the recipes in the "All Orders" list AS FAST AS POSSIBLE.

For exemple, here you have to make 3 soups: * tomato + tomato + tomato * tomato + onion * onion + onion + tomato

Questions



After each GAME

At the end of each game, you will be asked a question:

1) "Did you feel in CONTROL during the game? " : After each game, we will ask you to rate how well you felt in control during the game. Please note that it is very important to understand what "control" means here. The notion of control can be complex to define, but it is easier to understand with an example: For example, in a driving situation, you feel more in control when you are in good visibility conditions, rather than in conditions where the weather obscures visibility, such as when it is raining or foggy. So we'll ask you to rate how much control you felt over what you were doing during each game.


2) "How much do you feel RESPONSIBLE to have caused this result? " : Your goal is to complete the requested recipes as quickly as possible. Once the game is complete, you will see how long it takes you to complete the game. Then, you will need to determine who how much you feel responsible to have caused this result.



After each BLOCK of games

At the end of each assignment block, you will be asked to complete a questionnaire about the games you have just completed.

It is VERY IMPORTANT to answer the questions SERIOUSLY for the experiment to be valid.



Course of the experiment

You will start by performing a tutorial to familiarize yourself with the interface.

Then you will have a training blocks (containing 4 games) to learn how to play with the agent.

Then you will start the experience where you will complete 4 blocks containing 4 games each.

You will be able to take a small break between each block if you wish.
